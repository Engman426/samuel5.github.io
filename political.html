President Biden Announces New Infrastructure Plan

WASHINGTON - President Biden announced a new infrastructure plan today that would invest $2 trillion in the country's roads, bridges, and public transportation systems. The plan is part of the president's efforts to create jobs and stimulate the economy in the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic.

The plan includes funding for improvements to public transportation, as well as investments in renewable energy, broadband, and water infrastructure. It also includes funding for the construction of new affordable housing units and improvements to existing housing stock.

Republican leaders in Congress have criticized the plan, calling it too expensive and a burden on taxpayers. Democrats have praised the plan as a necessary investment in the country's infrastructure and economic future.

Supreme Court Strikes Down Voting Rights Act Provision

WASHINGTON - The U.S. Supreme Court today struck down a provision of the Voting Rights Act that required states with a history of voter discrimination to get federal approval before making changes to their voting laws.

The decision was met with criticism from civil rights groups and Democratic lawmakers, who argued that the provision was necessary to prevent voter suppression. Republican leaders hailed the decision as a victory for states' rights.

The Voting Rights Act was passed in 1965 to protect the voting rights of African Americans and other minority groups. The provision that was struck down by the Supreme Court had been used to block voting restrictions in several states, including Texas and North Carolina.
